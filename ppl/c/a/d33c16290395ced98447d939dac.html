Narrative

Samuel Bridge apparently rose within the working class of early 19th-century Manchester to become a builder capable of hiring several workers. Born at the end of the strife caused by the Napoleonic Wars, he must have witnessed unparalleled growth of the Manchester region, and no doubt benefitted and suffered from that growth.

Born about 1814, Samuel probably grew up in a poorly educated working class family, as he held the manual occupation of a "joiner" in 1845. Joiners were specialized construction workers who worked with wood fittings in house construction. By fifteen years later, in 1861, Samuel had risen within the profession to title himself as a "builder" who employed six men. No doubt the swelling economy of the Manchester region, one of the fastest growing industrial regions of England at the time, afforded him numerous clients for construction.

Samuel married Nancy Hanson probably around 1836.
