Narrative

Anne Williams was born in Virginia in 1810. Her lifetime would have witnessed the expansion of the country into the midwest prairies, the growth of the railroad and the market economy that the railroad encouraged, the Civil War, and the coming of electric inventions such as the telegraph and the phonograph. Living most of her life in Missouri, she would have known first-hand the sectional strife over slavery and states' rights that would rip the country apart during the Civil War. Although her husband did not fight in the war, her son, Philip Austin Thompson, rose to be a colonel in the Union army.

Anne's family moved from Virginia sometime during her childhood to St Louis county, Missouri. There is some confusion about Anne's surname, as it is sometimes reported as Darting, Dorting, or Williams. Because she married David Hamilton when she was only 17, it seems unlikely that she had a previous husband, although this possibility could account for the name confusion. Perhaps her father died young and her mother remarried, giving the Anne and her siblings a new surname. We don't know.

On 12 July 1827, when she was 17, Anne married David Hamilton Thompson, a man three years older than she, who was the son of Irish immigrants. Together, David and Anne had a total of nine children.

By 1830 David and Anne had a farm of their own in Callaway county, Missouri. By 1840, they had moved west to Buchanan county in the Platte Purchase land that had been added to western Missouri in 1837. Likely, they were lured by the chance at cheap farm land in this newly opened area at a time when land speculation was nearly a national pastime. By 1850, David and Anne had moved to farmland in Holt county, even further to the west in Missouri, again perhaps lured by the profits to be had in selling their existing farm and buying a new one.

Anne died in 1887 at the age of 77 in Templeton, Missouri.
