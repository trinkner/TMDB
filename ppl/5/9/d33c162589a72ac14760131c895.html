Narrative

Thomas Baldwin Was born in Virginia colony. He was a Major in the Revolutionary War and was given a piece of land for his services, as an officer in the Army, in Frederick County, VA, 7 miles east of Winchester, VA. He had two sons and three daughters. Rebecca Baldwin was born March 4, 1773, and died April 16, 1851.

Major Thomas Baldwin was born Sept. 2, 1746 and died August 14, 1809, in Frederick County. He was buried on his farm. Prior to 1770, he married Elizabeth. Thomas acquired a 500-acre tract from his father, William Baldwin, by deed dated March 4, 1783. This land was located in present-day Frederick County. Thomas served in the Revolutionary War, commanding an expedition in General Harmar' s campaign on the Ohio. Thomas apparently lived all his life in Frederick County... "

Thomas had two sons, William and Thomas, and five daughters. Rebecca,born March 4, 1773, married Hugh Martin, and was a widow by 1809; Lydia (Baldwin) Mooney died prior to 1809; Jane married (George?) Gordon;Mary "probably" married John Cox on August 6, 1790; and Eli Elizabeth married John Dunn.

"The Baldwins From Virginia Westward" is recommended to anyone wishing to learn more about this family. on page 14 the author tells of Elizabeth's first cousin, another Elizabeth Baldwin, who around 1790 was abducted by Indians in Virginia and taken eventually to Detroit, where two of her brothers found her. "They made a harrowing trip home by canoe and across country, sometimes going without food for as long as one week at a time". She had been gone four years.
