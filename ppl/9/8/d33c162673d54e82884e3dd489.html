Narrative

John Hipple was born 10 March 1759 in Pikeland, Chester, Pennsylvania and died 27 December 1841 in Woodbury Township, Bedford, Pennsylvania. He married Ann Dilts, daughter of John Dilts and Margaret Hector. She was born about 1760 in New Jersey, and died 27 December 1841 in Woodbury.

John, living east of Waterside, supplied tar to all the community. He burned it in specially prepared kilns from the rich pine to be found on Tussey Mountain. Tar was used in wagon making and about the farm for medicinal purposes. If an animal received an injury, tar was applied to the wound. He worked as a farmer at Morrison's Cove, Woodbury, and as a supplier of pine tar, also at Morrison's Cove. He served as a private in Captain Parker's Chester County Militia.

John apparently owned three tracts of land, one of 189 acres, one of 200 acres, and a third of 280 acres.

He served as a private in Captain Parker's company of the Chester County Militia.

Hipple's Cave is located on land where homestead was built. Hipple's Cave was a popular tourist attraction in the early 1900's for reunions and picnics.
