Narrative

Carl Christian "Charles" Trinkner was born 28 Apr 1846 in the small farming village of Freudental, Neckarkreis, Kingdom of Württemberg to his parents, Andreas Jacob Trinkner and Maria Elisabetha Rosine "Mary" Schwarz. He emigrated from Württemberg in May, 1865 and came to the United States.

In December 1866, Charles enlisted in the US Army, just a year after the end of the US Civil War. He registered as a wagon maker and served in three infantry divisions until he was discharged at the end of his enlistment term in November 1869. His discharge took place at Camp Grant, in the Arizona Territory. During this time, his uncle, Johann Michael "Michael" Trinkner had moved his family from Freudental to Cleveland. Charles must have liked Arizona enough to stay there, rather than to unite with his uncle and cousins in Ohio. In 1877, Charles's father also moved from Freudental to Cleveland, bringing with him his wife and children (Charles's siblings). In June, 1884, Charles received a homestead title to land just northeast of the present-day town center of Florence, Arizona.

Charles apparently first worked as a farm laborer in Arizona. In 1875, he married Francisca Rodríguez who was born in Mexico. They had five girls between 1876 and 1889: Guadalupe, Louise, Francisca ("Frankie"), Victoria, and Carolina ("Carrie"). Charles's wife, Francisca, apparently died in 1890.

In 1892, Charles married Pasquale Velásquez. They had no children together. By the 1900 census, Charles had become the owner of a livestock ranch in Pinal county, where he apprently would work as a rancher for the rest of this life. He must have been active in his community, as in 1896 he was a member of the Pinal school board.

Charles likely spoke three languages: German, Spanish, and English. Both his wives were born in Mexico, and likely spoke Spanish with him in their home. His daughters married Latino men and named their children with Spanish first names. Since he was on the school board, he likely spoke fluent English. And, of course, he was raised in Germany, so he spoke German.

In 1899, Charles was shot and dangerously wounded on his ranch by a man named John Hall. Charles recovered, and lived until 1908, when he died in Pinal.
