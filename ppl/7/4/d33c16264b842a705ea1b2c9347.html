Narrative

William Baldwin was likely the son of Thomas Baldwin. He was born in 1716 near Philadelphia, according to the statements of two of his descendants. He died in 1785 in Berkeley County, in what is now know as West Virginia, where his will was written September 7, and probated October 18, 1785. He married twice and became the father of at least 14 children. His first wife was Mary, who must have died between 1759 and 1773. His second wife was Jane Hedges, who survived him.

In the late 1740s William and his brother, Francis, came to a part of Frederick County that later became Berkeley County, West Virginia. They "obtained grants of land on opposite sides of Back Creek. Their names appear as chain-carriers on each other's surveys in 1751."
