Narrative

Catherine and her husband, William Ephraim Leach, lived a long while in Canton, Illinois, and had 10 children, eight of whom survived. They were devoted members of their church, and well remembered in the community.

A mystery lies in the second marriage of W.E. Leach, to his childhood sweetheart Drucilla Donihue, whom he married after Catherine died. The mystery lies in how the relationship between W.E. and Catherine ended. In 1900, Catherine Leach was reported on the federal census in the federal census for Galesburg, Illinois as the "head" of her household, which included herself, her son William H., her dauhter Susie, and Susie's husband, Frank O. Anderson. W.E. does not appear in the listing. In 1900 Catherine was 59 years old. On the census, she is listed as a widow, which would imply that W.E. had died. We know that he did not die until 1908, and that he married Drucilla in 1904.

So, what happened? Did William leave Catherine in search of his life-long love? Did they agree to separate? Did Catherine leave him?

The newspaper clippings of William's marriage to Drucilla are a lovely story of two lovers who were refused marriage in their youth because Drucilla's father believed that William was beneath Drucilla's station. Drucilla evidently saved herself for William throughout her life, when finally they were married and lived out their few remaining years together as upstanding members of the Michigan society. But what of Catherine? And why did William not stay with Catherine throughout her final years? Why did Catherine consider him dead when she reported herself as a widow in 1900?

Catherine Martin Hipple, died in 1901. The following article appeared in the Canton newspaper.

December 4, 1901

A message received by Mrs. G.W. Scott, announces the death of her mother, Mrs. W.E. Leach, which occurred in Galesburg Wednesday evening. Mrs. Leach is well known in Canton and the news causes sincere grief. She was the daughter of Dr. and Mrs. Martin, former residents of Canton. The family moved to Kansas about 20 years ago, where her mother is still living. She leaves children as follows: Mrs. G.W. Scott, Canton; Mrs. Frank Anderson and Mrs. H.M. Tompkins of Galesburg; Mrs. Frank Long, Cincinnati; Mrs. Lester Stevens, Kansas; Jennie, Will and Edith, Galesburg.

She was 55 years of age and her husband will be recalled as station agent here. She has been a sufferer from heart trouble for a long time, but her demise was unexpected. The remains will be brought to this city for burial, the funeral probably taking place from the Methodist Church, of which denomination she was a devout member.
