Occupation     Court Administrator (Gerichtswerwandter)  
General

Translation, from friend Steffen Meyer:

Regarding your German term, it appears to be an archaic term (~ pre 1850, depending on the state) for a member or administrator of a judicial court, typically on behalf of the lord of the state, who might not have the judicial qualifications to take care of daily judicial business him-/herself, despite being technically the last appeal (court = "das Gericht," associate/member/relative = "der Verwandte").
I'm not sure on the following, but due to differences in regional dialects, the "Gerichtsverwandte" may be equivalent to the "Gerichtsverwalter" (court administrator) or "Gerichtshalter" (court holder) in other parts of Germany.
The Gerichtsverwandte would have focused on what was called "lower" law, i.e. property law, family law, inheritence law, some contract law, and misdemeanor-level criminal law.

Data sources: The basic translation of the term is mine. The descriptions are mostly from the following two German wikipedia articles. I tend to be skeptical of wikipedia, but this fits in with what else I know about the period, and it's not a controversial topic, so I feel confident that the description is pretty good:
