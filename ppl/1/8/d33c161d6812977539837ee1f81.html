Narrative

Daniel Ephraim Leach, was born in New York state in 1810. He enlisted in the army at the age of 19 at Rochester, New York, and remained in the army until he was 33, in 1843. Between 1829 and 1833, he was station at Fort Gratiot, St. Clair County, Michigan. For the next ten years, he was stationed at Fort Brady, Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan, serving as an Ordnance Sergeant . He left the military with the rank of 1st Lieutenant, and was the officer in charge of Fort Brady.

After his military service, Daniel turned to farming. He remained in Michgan, and owned property in China Township, St Clair County. He married Agnes Baird, probably before 1831, and lived with her throughout his adult life. She was a Scottish immigrant who had come with her family to the utopian Jones Colony in Upper Canada (present-day Ontario). Unfortunately, the colony failed and many of its members, including Agnes's family, crossed the border to settle in Michigan.

Daniel and Agnes had seven children, starting in 1831. They lived the rest of their lives in China Township.

When the Civil War started, Daniel once again entered the military. He was commissioned as a 1st Lieutenant for the 10th Michigan Infantry, Company E, when the unit was first organized in 1861, and signed on for three years, at the age of 51. The regiment left Michigan on April 22, 1862, under command of Colonel Charles M. Lam, and joined the army under General Hallock, at Pittsburg Landing, Tenn., and was assigned to the Second Brigade, General Pope's Division.

His company confronted the confederates the moment it reached its destination, and at once began a series of marches and skirmishes that occupied every day, together with the hard work of building trenches and fortifications. When confederate forces retreated from Corinth, his regiment went into camp at Big Springs, near present-day Corinth, Mississippi. On July 7, 1862, after fighting for eight months, Daniel died of disease. He was 52. His wife, Agnes, died three years later in 1865 at the age of 54.
