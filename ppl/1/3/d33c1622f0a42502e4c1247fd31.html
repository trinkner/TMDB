Narrative

Egisto Binda likely emigrated from Italy and entered the US in 1898. He likely then moved to Canada, where Herbert and Andrew were born. He may have lived in Canada, as an Egisto Binda lived in Quebec in 1891.

The family likely then moved back to US. A record exists of Egisto sailing to the United States on the ship "La Savoie," but on the passenger list, he is listed as already a U.S. national. As he reentered the US via Ellis Island, his passenger statement indicated that he already lived in the US, and wasn't immigrating for the first time to the US.

Evidently, the family was not proud of its Italian roots, and so sons Alberto and Amadeo anglicized their names to Herbert and Andrew. The birth dates of the two sons fits what we know about their births, and the two names are very close to their English names. No other Egisto Binda can be found anywhere on the Internet, so we can be reasonably confident that this Egisto is in fact the Egisto of our family. The birth date fits him in all three documents I've found.

According to Louise Binda, Egisto's granddaughter, originally Egisto and Alinda Serafini came from Florence. She said that Alinda was in a convent school and ran away to marry Egisto.

Egisto died before Alinda, and she opened a boarding house in the Boston area. It was then that she had an affair with one of her boarders and produced her illegitimate children Freddie and Marie.
